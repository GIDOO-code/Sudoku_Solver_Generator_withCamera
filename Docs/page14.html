Latin squares is a Matrix with numbers 1 to n arranged vertically and horizontally without overlapping.
Sudoku's solution is that it divides the 9×9 Latin square into 3×3 blocks and places numbers 1 to 9 on the block without overlapping. There is 5.525×1027 for the 9×9 Latin square(5524751496156892842531225600). The 9×9 Latin square with block constraint(Sudoku's solution) is 6.671×1021 and about 1/1,000,000 (6670903752021072936960).

reference: Frazer Jarvis, June 20, 2005,Enumerating possible Sudoku grids,
　　　http://www.afjarvis.staff.shef.ac.uk/Sudoku/Sudoku.pdf
